Focus on the positive.

Just like anyone else, children with autism spectrum disorder often respond well to positive reinforcement. That means when you praise them for the behaviors they’re doing well, it will make them (and you) feel good.

Attention to Detail

Many children with autism have a keen eye for detail and are able to notice things that others might overlook

Memory Skills

Some children with autism have exceptional memory skills, particularly in areas of interest.

Visual Thinking

Many individuals with autism think in a visual manner, which can be beneficial for tasks that involve spatial reasoning, creativity, or problem-solving.

Analytical Thinking

Children with autism often excel in analytical thinking and may approach problems in unconventional ways.

Learn More About ASD?

Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) is a neurological and developmental disorder that affects how people interact with others, communicate, learn, and behave. Although autism can be diagnosed at any age, it is described as a “developmental disorder” because symptoms generally appear in the first 2 years of life.
